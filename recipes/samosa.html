Samosa

Description

Flaky and crunchy fried samosa are one of the most popular street food snack in North Indian cuisine. They feature a pastry-like crust but are filled with savory and spiced potato and green peas for a hearty, delicious snack.

Ingredients

  • 250 g all-prpose flour
  • 60 ml oil
  • 90 ml water
  • 3.7 g carom seeds
  • 3.7 g salt
  • 500 g potatoes
  • 72.5 g green peas
  • 15 g oil
  • 15 g ginger minced or paste
  • 1 to 2 green chillies chopped
  • 1 pinch hinge
  • 4 tablespoons coriander leaves chopped finely
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 2.46 g salt
  • 3.7 g cumin seeds
  • 3.7 to 5 g garam masala
  • 3.7 g red chilli powder
  • 2.5 g cumin powder
  • 2.5 g fennel powder

Steps

  1. Boil potatoes just until done without making mushy.
  2. Crumble them, do not mash them up. Set aside.
  3. Mix together flour, carom seeds, salt and oil in a mixing bowl.
  4. Rub the flour well for 2 to 3 minutes in between the palms to incorporate the oil well.
  5. Take handful of flour and press down with your fingers
  6. Add water little by little and mix the flour to form a stiff dough. Cover and rest for 25 to 30 mins.
  7. Heat a pan with oil. Add cumin seeds.
  8. When they begin to sizzle, add ginger & green chillies. Fry until ginger smells good, for about 30 to 60 seconds.
  9. Next add green peas and saute for 2 mins.
  10. Add red chilli powder, garam masala, cumin powder & fennel powder. Saute for 30 seconds.
  11. Next add potatoes and sprinkle salt. Mix and saute for 2 to 3 mins. Mix in coriander leaves and set aside to cool.
  12. Taste and add more salt if needed. if using lemon juice, add it now.
  13. Knead the dough gently to smoothen a bit. Make 5 portions and roll to balls. Cover the dough.
  14. Grease the rolling area and then flatten a ball. Drizzle some oil.
  15. Begin to roll to oval shape of 8.5 inches long by 6.5 inches wide.
  16. Cut it to 2 parts. The 2 parts make 2 samosas. If the edges are too thick, gently roll it to thin down.
  17. Take one part and apply water over the straight edge. Join the edges to make a cone. Press down gently to seal the cone from inside as well.
  18. Fill the cone with potato masala and press down with your finger to push it inside the cone.
  19. Bring the edges together and seal them by pinching off the edges together.
  20. After you make 5 samosas, begin to heat the oil until medium hot.
  21. Gently add as many samosas as you can to the oil and fry them on a low flame undisturbed for few minutes.
  22. When the crust becomes firm, increase the flame to medium. Rotate them to the other side and fry until crispy and golden.
